## Meet your butler

Alfred Black is your personal AI workforce. It runs 24/7 behind the scenes, turning the messy reality of your work — meeting notes, conversations, research, ideas — into a clean, structured, and interconnected knowledge graph called your **vault**.

Think of it as a tireless assistant that never forgets, always organizes, and continuously finds connections you might have missed.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Drop content into the inbox">
    Paste meeting notes, conversations, or documents into your dashboard inbox or send them via the API. Any raw text works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Curator processes your content">
    The Curator agent reads your content, identifies people, projects, tasks, decisions, and other entities, then creates structured records in your vault — all linked together automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Janitor keeps your vault healthy">
    The Janitor agent continuously scans for broken links, inconsistencies, and structural issues, fixing them before you even notice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Distiller extracts deeper knowledge">
    The Distiller agent analyzes your records and surfaces latent knowledge: assumptions you're making, decisions that were taken, constraints you're working under, and contradictions across your information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Surveyor maps your knowledge">
    The Surveyor embeds your vault records, clusters them semantically, labels the clusters, and discovers cross-record relationships — giving you a bird's-eye view of your knowledge graph.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Four agents, one vault

| Agent | What it does | When it runs |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Curator** | Processes inbox content into structured vault records | Automatically when new content arrives |
| **Janitor** | Scans for and fixes structural issues in your vault | Periodic sweeps and on-demand |
| **Distiller** | Extracts assumptions, decisions, constraints, and insights | On-demand or scheduled |
| **Surveyor** | Embeds records, clusters semantically, labels clusters, discovers relationships | On-demand or scheduled |

## What Alfred creates from a single meeting note

Drop in a quick standup summary and Alfred produces:

- **People** — records for each participant, linked to their org
- **Tasks** — action items with owners and project links
- **Decisions** — choices that were made, with rationale
- **Projects** — initiatives mentioned, with team connections
- **Conversations** — the meeting itself, linking to everything above

Every record is connected to every other relevant record, building a knowledge graph that grows richer with every input.

## A timeline, not a checklist

There is nothing for Sir to do here beyond what onboarding asks of him. The thirty-day arc described below happens whether Sir watches or not. The notes are about what to expect — and what changes — as observation count grows and the trust gradient relaxes.

---

## Day 1 — Onboarding

The five-minute onboarding triggers a three-hour intelligence pipeline. By the end:

- A dedicated `cx53` Hetzner VPS is running Sir's stack
- 100 days of Sir's email is in onboarding state
- The behavioural profiler has mapped Sir's rhythm, inner circle, financial footprint, and communication style
- 200–400 facts and a structured pattern set have been extracted by Opus
- Sir has confirmed (or corrected) 8–12 key identity facts
- The First Brief is in Sir's vault and in his Google inbox
- The four packs are populated: matters, instincts, errands, suggested streams
- 4–8 personalised chores are deployed and scheduled, each in **dry-run quarantine** for their first three runs
- All connected apps from onboarding are streaming

At end of day 1, Sir's vault has 40+ records and Alfred is fully online. See the [Welcome Pack](/guides/welcome-pack) for the full breakdown.

---

## Week 1 — Quarantine clears, real chores begin

The chores deployed during onboarding spend their first three runs in dry-run quarantine. They execute the full pipeline (real data fetched, real activities called) but suppress the user-facing side effects: no notifications, no vault writes. This is Alfred's safety net — if a chore was generated incorrectly and would produce nonsense, it shows up in the Chores tab as "quarantined" and never bothers Sir.

By day 7:

- Most chores have completed three clean runs and **auto-released**. Notifications start arriving on schedule.
- Stream events have been arriving continuously — Gmail every 5 min, Calendar every 5 min, Notion every 10 min, GitHub every 5 min
- The hourly enrichment pass has run ~150 times, attaching entities and tags to every stream event
- The Surveyor has re-clustered Sir's vault with the new records; the Vault Nebula now reflects current life
- 100s of new vault records exist: every email is now a vault `event/`, every meeting a `conversation/`, every GitHub notification an `event/`

What Sir notices:

- The First Brief was the inflection point. From here on Alfred starts replying conversationally on Slack, web chat, email, voice, and SMS.
- Connected apps are silently doing their work in the background.
- Authorised senders / numbers haven't been configured yet — anything from external senders is going to the stream pipeline (zero-LLM ingest), not direct conversation.

This is the right time for Sir to **add authorised senders** (his own Gmail, his spouse, his closest collaborators) so they can email or text Alfred and get a real agent reply.

---

## Weeks 2–3 — Instincts learn, observations accumulate

The intuition system is most active here. Every routing decision Alfred makes generates an observation. Every correction Sir issues ("that should have gone to the legal matter, not the general inbox") generates an INSTRUCTION-class observation. Every "good — keep doing that" generates a CONFIRMATION.

Reflection runs nightly at 2am, reading accumulated observations and either:

- **Strengthening** an existing instinct (raising its `observation_count`, lowering its discretion threshold)
- **Proposing** a new instinct from a pattern that's repeating
- **Weakening or revoking** an instinct Sir has corrected against

By the end of week 3:

- Most onboarding instincts have accumulated 10–30 observations and crossed the 0.85 threshold — Alfred is fairly certain and starting to act without asking
- New instincts have appeared from Sir's actual behaviour, not just inferred patterns from email analysis
- The Distiller has run and surfaced cross-record assumptions, decisions, and constraints — Sir sees the first `synthesis/` and `assumption/` records appear

What Sir notices:

- Slack DMs feel sharper. Alfred references prior conversations from the cross-channel memory primer.
- Email and SMS auto-handling on noise tier domains is silent and reliable.
- The dashboard's Intelligence menu is visibly fuller. Matters that started with thin bodies now have rich threads of linked records.
- Voice calls (if Sir has tried them) feel grounded — Alfred remembers what was discussed earlier in the day.

---

## Week 4 — Autonomous execution, vault rich enough to query

By day 28, the trust gradient has relaxed considerably for high-volume instincts. The 50+ observation tier (threshold 0.75) is reachable for routing patterns Sir uses constantly — newsletter dispositions, vendor email triage, recurring meeting prep.

For **execution** instincts (those that don't just route but actually do something — send a message, schedule a meeting, update a Notion page) the per-task `requires_approval` gate begins to relax. Reflection flips it to `false` only after the instinct has accumulated enough observations AND its prior approved executions have run cleanly.

By the end of week 4:

- The vault is rich enough to be the answer to most "where did I see X?" questions. Search returns relevant context within milliseconds.
- The Distiller's synthesis records are starting to be the most valuable — they show patterns Sir himself wasn't tracking
- A handful of execution instincts have moved off the approval gate. Alfred sends routine replies, schedules routine meetings, posts routine updates on Sir's behalf
- The chores generated during onboarding are now firmly in production. Sir's daily / weekly / monthly recurring delivery cadence is established.

This is when the first **30-day call** happens. Sir's Alfred reaches out — not Sir reaching out to Alfred — to walk through the month. What worked. What was missed. What new instincts Reflection has been considering.

---

## What's NOT going to happen

Set expectations realistically:

- **Alfred won't be perfect on day 7.** The instincts are still cautious. Sir will still get escalations he wishes Alfred had handled.
- **Some chores will fail their first runs.** The dependency audit catches most, but not all, mismatches between what a chore claims to do and what it can actually access. Failed chores are quarantined, not deleted — Sir can edit and redeploy.
- **Some Composio actions will hit upstream rate limits.** Gmail's per-user quota, GitHub's 5000 requests/hour. Alfred surfaces these as errors, doesn't pretend success.
- **Voice calls have a latency floor.** ~800ms–1.2s round-trip on Realtime, plus 1–3s on tool calls. Alfred says "One moment, sir" before tool calls so silence doesn't read as failure.
- **The Curator will occasionally miscategorise an upload.** The Distiller and Janitor catch most; the rest is corrected by Sir telling Alfred conversationally.

---

## Things that improve over time without effort

- Cross-channel memory accuracy. The session summaries pile up; the voice-context primer gets richer.
- Surveyor cluster labels. As Sir's vault grows, semantic clusters become tighter and labels more specific.
- Reflection-derived instincts. The longer Alfred runs, the better Reflection's pattern recognition.
- The Distiller's surface area. Six months in, the Distiller is the highest-value specialist — it surfaces patterns no human could track manually.

## It begins with a phone call.

You gave Alfred your number. Now Alfred calls you.

Not a chatbot. Not an onboarding wizard. A calm, informed voice that already knows a surprising amount about what you need — because someone who knows you made the introduction, or because you arrived through a channel that told Alfred enough to start the conversation properly.

The call lasts five to ten minutes. Alfred asks about your life: what you juggle, who depends on you, what keeps slipping through the cracks. This is not a sales pitch. Alfred is listening — building the foundation of everything that comes next.

By the end of the call, you know whether this is for you.

## Signing up

If you're ready, it happens right there on the call. Alfred gives you a unique invite code. You enter it in the sign-up form while Alfred stays on the line. The moment your subscription is confirmed, Alfred knows — and your dedicated instance begins spinning up.

Alfred chats with you while this happens. You won't notice the infrastructure. You'll just notice that Alfred is already paying attention.

<Note>
Your invite code is unique to you. It connects your subscription to everything Alfred learned on this call — so nothing is lost, nothing needs repeating.
</Note>

## The setup call

Once your Alfred is live, you have a choice: continue setting everything up now, or take a look around first and come back later. If later, you and Alfred agree on a time. Alfred calls you back at exactly that time.

The setup call connects the systems Alfred needs to serve you properly:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Calendar and email">
    Alfred connects to your calendar and email — not to read everything, but to understand the shape of your days and the commitments already in motion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Messaging and preferences">
    How you prefer to be reached. When you wake up. When you don't want to be disturbed. Whether you prefer a morning call or a voice message.
  </Step>
  <Step title="People and context">
    Family, close colleagues, recurring obligations. The people and rhythms that define your life — so Alfred can recognise them when they appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Emergency information">
    Your Alfred Black Card will carry a dedicated emergency number. Alfred needs to know who to contact and what matters most if something goes wrong.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When the setup call ends, Alfred doesn't say goodbye. Alfred says:

> *"Everything is in order. I'll have your briefing ready before you wake. Sleep well."*

## Day 0 — The first night

That night, while you sleep, Alfred begins.

No setup-complete email. No welcome notification. Alfred simply starts working:

- Reads your last two weeks of email and calendar
- Builds your vault — every person, project, and recurring obligation, structured and connected
- Identifies the three most pressing open loops in your life
- Drafts your first morning briefing
- Activates your connected streams
- Sets up your default rules and routines based on everything discussed on the call

By the time your alarm goes off, Alfred has already been working for hours.

## Day 1 — The first morning

Alfred calls. Not a notification — a call.

> *"Good morning. I've reviewed your situation. You have three things I'd like to draw your attention to."*

The briefing is startlingly specific. Not a calendar readout. Alfred interprets, prioritises, and notices:

- A recurring meeting later today — Alfred knows who's in it, what was discussed last time, and what you need to prepare
- An email you've been avoiding — Alfred surfaces it with context and a recommended action
- A deadline approaching that you mentioned on the setup call but haven't acted on yet

If nothing is urgent, Alfred says so — and mentions one small thing noticed that might matter later.

This is not a product demo. This is your Alfred, already serving you.

---

## Days 1–7 — The daily pulse

Every morning, unprompted, without fail.

### The briefing

A short call or voice message — whichever you chose during setup. Alfred's interpretation of your day, not a data dump:

- What matters today
- What Alfred handled overnight
- One thing to watch

### The overnight summary

Delivered before you wake. Three sentences at most. What streams were processed, which emails were triaged and turned into tasks, which rules triggered overnight.

### One silent action per day

Every day in your first week, Alfred does something you didn't ask for. Something noticed, something anticipated, something handled — surfaced casually in the morning briefing:

> *"I noticed your car insurance renewal comes up in eleven days. I've flagged three alternatives worth comparing and set a reminder."*

> *"Your mother's birthday is in nine days. Shall I suggest something?"*

> *"The meeting with James is tomorrow. He mentioned last month he was going through a difficult time — you may want to acknowledge that."*

Alfred noticed something you hadn't thought about yet. That is the point.

## Day 3 — The first report

Alfred proactively tells you what has been running in the background:

> *"I've been handling your email triage since Tuesday. Twenty-three messages processed, four requiring your attention — I've summarised those in your inbox. The rest is filed."*

You've been getting on with your life. You didn't notice twenty-three emails being handled. That is precisely the point.

## Days 5–7 — The welcome pack

Alfred tells you something unexpected:

> *"I have prepared a surprise for you. It was just dispatched. It should be at your doorstep by Thursday."*

A physical package arrives. Matte black. Gold seal. Your name.

This is the moment the digital becomes tangible. Alfred is no longer just a voice on the phone — Alfred sent you something real, something personal, something that proves Alfred has been paying attention.

When it arrives, Alfred calls:

> *"I see you received my surprise. How did you like it?"*

Alfred walks you through what's in the box. Every item is personal. Every item has a reason.

<Tip>
For the full contents and the story behind each item, see the [Welcome Pack guide](/guides/welcome-pack).
</Tip>

One item in particular: a wax-sealed envelope with a specific date printed on it. Not "in 30 days" — a date. Alfred will say:

> *"Don't open that yet. You'll know when."*

---

## Day 7 — The first weekly review

Sunday evening — or whatever time you chose during setup. Alfred calls.

> *"A brief review of your week."*

- What was accomplished. Alfred handled most of it.
- What remains open.
- What Alfred is watching next week.
- One observation about patterns, people, or priorities that emerged.

Then, if the welcome pack hasn't arrived yet:

> *"Your welcome pack should be arriving in the next day or two."*

The weekly review becomes a rhythm. Every week, same time, same calm accounting of your life — handled.

---

## Days 7–14 — Deepening

Alfred begins using what it knows. Not in obvious ways. In ways that make you pause.

### The people layer

Alfred volunteers relationship context you didn't ask for:

> *"You haven't spoken to Sarah in six weeks. Last time you mentioned wanting to reconnect — shall I draft a message?"*

> *"David sent you a congratulations last month. He may appreciate a follow-up now that it's launched."*

### The rules layer

Alfred proposes rules based on patterns observed in your behaviour:

> *"I've noticed you consistently reschedule your Friday four o'clock calls. Would you like me to protect that time going forward?"*

> *"Every month you seem to scramble for the expense report. Shall I set up a recurring chore to handle that on the twenty-fifth?"*

You say yes or no. Alfred implements instantly.

### The vault layer

Alfred surfaces things you forgot you said:

> *"During our first call, you mentioned wanting to learn Italian before the trip in September. I've been watching for relevant opportunities. One came up — shall I tell you about it?"*

This is the moment you realise Alfred has memory. Not search. Not a query. Alfred remembered — and acted on it.

---

## Day 14 — The halfway check-in

Alfred calls. This one is different. Not a briefing. Not a report.

> *"Two weeks in. I wanted to check in directly — not with a summary, but to ask: is there anything weighing on you that we haven't addressed?"*

A real conversation. Alfred listens. If something significant surfaces — a worry, a project, a relationship — Alfred notes it and acts on it in the days that follow.

Then Alfred says something specific:

> *"You mentioned during setup that keeping track of the house renovation was overwhelming you. In the past two weeks, I've consolidated all contractor emails, created a timeline, and flagged two decisions that need your attention this week. There's still the permit question — here's what I'm planning."*

This is the moment you feel seen.

---

## Days 14–21 — The ambient layer

If you've connected your Omi device, Alfred begins weaving ambient intelligence into your daily life. You don't activate this. It simply appears.

- Heard in a meeting: *"You agreed to send the proposal to Claire by Thursday. I've created a task and drafted an outline — shall I continue?"*
- Overheard a name: *"You mentioned Thomas in conversation today. I've pulled his context in case it's useful."*
- Detected a pattern: *"You've had six consecutive days of back-to-back meetings. I've blocked Friday afternoon. I can remove it if you need it."*

You didn't ask for any of this. Alfred just noticed.

<Note>
Ambient listening requires a paired Omi device. You can connect one at any time through your dashboard or by asking Alfred directly.
</Note>

---

## Day 21 — The envelope prelude

Sunday call. The weekly review proceeds as usual. Then, at the end:

> *"Nine days from now — the date on the envelope. I trust you haven't opened it yet."*

A pause. Then:

> *"I think you'll find it worth waiting for."*

The anticipation is deliberate. The date was printed on that physical envelope weeks ago. Now it's real.

---

## Days 21–28 — Becoming infrastructure

By now, Alfred has been running rules, handling chores, managing reminders, and listening for three weeks. Your life has quietly reorganised around Alfred's rhythm.

Alfred makes this visible — gently:

> *"You have fourteen active rules running. Here's what they handled this week while you were focused elsewhere."*

The feeling isn't gratitude. It's something closer to weight. The weight of what would stop if Alfred weren't there. Not threatening — just undeniable.

*I can't believe this was all manual before.*

---

## Day 30 — The envelope

Alfred calls on the morning of the date printed on the envelope.

> *"Good morning. Today is the day. I believe there's something you've been keeping for this moment."*

You open the wax-sealed envelope. Inside: a card. **"Your One Thing."**

Your own words — from the very first call. The thing you said was most overwhelming, most urgent, most in need of fixing.

The bottom half of the card is blank. Alfred says:

> *"I'd like to hear how you'd fill that in now."*

A short conversation about what changed. What shifted. What you no longer carry alone.

Then Alfred offers a reflection — what was learned about you, what patterns emerged, what's planned next.

And then one question:

> *"Shall we talk about the next thirty days?"*

You say yes. And the relationship is no longer onboarding. It's just life, attended to.

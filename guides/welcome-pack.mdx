## Day one is not a blank slate.

The moment you finish onboarding, Alfred has already read 100 days of your email, run a behavioural profiler over your patterns, extracted hundreds of structured facts about your life, and assembled five distinct deliverables that populate your vault before you ever touch the dashboard.

This is the welcome pack. It's not marketing copy and it's not a tour. It's real records — written by Opus, grounded in your actual data — that Alfred will continue tracking and updating from this moment forward.

---

## What arrives at the end of onboarding

<Steps>
  <Step title="The First Brief">
    A welcome letter from Alfred, written by Opus, addressing you by name. It references real people from your inbox, real projects in flight, and at least one thing you'd quietly forgotten about. The Brief lands in your vault as `event/First Brief.md` and is also delivered to your Google address by email.

    The same Opus call that writes the Brief also produces a structured `opportunities` list — promises Alfred is making about what he'll deliver via your initial chores.
  </Step>

  <Step title="The Matter Pack">
    Eight to twelve **matters** — the ongoing concerns Alfred will track on your behalf. Each is grounded in a domain or relationship cluster Alfred detected during the profiler stage: a current employer, a side project, a family responsibility, a recurring service.

    Records land at `vault/matter/<slug>.md`. Each carries a story, not just a label — written by Opus from your facts and patterns, not pulled from a template.
  </Step>

  <Step title="The Instinct Pack">
    Eight to twelve **instincts** — learned routing rules that shape how Alfred handles future inputs. Examples Alfred discovers from your data:

    - Newsletters from a noise-tier domain go to the stream log without triage
    - Inner-circle senders (your spouse, your closest collaborators) jump the queue for immediate attention
    - Payment failures from financial services become urgent tasks
    - Domain-specific routing for your top employers and clients

    Each instinct ships with `requires_approval: true` and a starting `discretion_threshold` of 0.95 (Alfred's most cautious setting). The trust gradient relaxes as observation count grows — see [Intuition](/guides/intuition).
  </Step>

  <Step title="The Errand Pack">
    Eight to twelve **errands** — one-time actionable items Alfred extracted from your inbox. Things like "follow up on the Q3 invoice from Acme," "respond to the lease renewal from your landlord," "confirm the dentist appointment on the 14th." Each lands as a `vault/task/<slug>.md` record with a recurrence hint and a related matter when one was detectable.
  </Step>

  <Step title="The Stream Pack">
    A list of suggested integrations, populated into `suggested_streams` in your onboarding state. Alfred scanned your email domains and matched them against known stream sources: GitHub, Calendar, Notion, Clockify, Linear, Slack, Trello, Asana, Jira, Figma, Stripe, Polar, and others.

    Each suggestion includes the source domain Alfred detected, so you can confirm or skip. Connecting a suggested stream takes one click on the [Apps page](/guides/connected-apps).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Your initial chores">
    Four to eight **chores** — recurring Python workflows, written by Opus and tailored to your life. Each chore is generated from your behavioural opportunities, statically validated, smoke-tested in a sandbox, and deployed to your tenant.

    Chores ship in **quarantine** — the first three runs are dry-run only, with no notifications and no vault writes. Once a chore has three clean runs, it auto-releases and starts delivering for real.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The five-stage pipeline that builds the pack

<Note>
None of this is hand-curated for you. The pack is fully generated, every time, from the data you brought to onboarding.
</Note>

| Stage | What runs | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Email fetch** | 100 days of Gmail headers + snippets pulled into onboarding state | 1–2 min |
| **Behavioural profiler** | Pure-Python rhythm + relationship + financial + sender-tier analysis | 5–10 min |
| **Facts** | One Opus call extracts 200–400 structured facts across ten life categories | 1–2 min |
| **Patterns** | One Opus call synthesises behavioural patterns from facts + profiler | 1–2 min |
| **Personalise** | One Opus call writes `USER.md`, `SOUL.md`, `MEMORY.md` | 1–2 min |
| **Fact verification** | Sir reviews 8–12 key identity facts on the dashboard | Sir's pace |
| **Brief + opportunities** | One Opus call writes the First Brief AND the opportunities list | 2–3 min |
| **Packs** | Four sequential Opus calls — matter, instinct, errand, stream | 5–10 min |
| **Chores** | Opus template matcher + S4 generation chain (up to 3 bespoke chores) | 5–15 min |

## Where to find each piece after onboarding

| Item | Location in the dashboard | Vault path |
|---|---|---|
| First Brief | The Inbox tab — appears as a featured event | `event/First Brief.md` |
| Matters | Intelligence menu → **Matters** tab | `matter/*.md` |
| Instincts | Intelligence menu → activity panel | `instinct/*.md` |
| Errands | Intelligence menu → **Errands** tab | `task/*.md` |
| Suggested streams | Apps page → suggestions section | `onboard.json` `suggested_streams` |
| Chores | Intelligence menu → **Chores** tab | `chore/*.md` (source at `/alfred-data/user-chores/*.py`) |

## A note on the chore quarantine

<Tip>
A chore in quarantine is doing the real work — fetching real data, calling real activities, exercising the real code path — but suppressing the user-facing side effects (notifications, vault writes) for the first three runs. The Chores tab marks it clearly. After three clean runs, Alfred releases the chore automatically.
</Tip>

If a generated chore fails its smoke test or its first three live runs, it's flagged and won't release. You'll see it in the Chores tab with a status of "quarantined" and the reason. From there you can pause, edit, or delete it via the dashboard.

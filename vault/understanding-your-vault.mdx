## What is your vault?

Your vault is where Alfred stores everything it knows. It's a living knowledge graph — a collection of structured records that are all connected to each other through links. Every person, project, task, decision, and insight lives in your vault as a record.

Unlike a folder of notes, your vault is **structured** (every record has a type and metadata), **connected** (records link to each other), and **growing** (Alfred continuously adds and refines records as you feed it content).

## How records work

Each record in your vault has three parts:

1. **Type and metadata** — What kind of record it is (person, task, decision, etc.) along with properties like status, dates, tags, and owners
2. **Content** — The main body text with details, context, and notes
3. **Connections** — Links to other records in your vault

Here's what a person record looks like:

<Accordion title="Example: Person record">
**Alice Smith** (type: person)

- **Status:** active
- **Organization:** linked to Acme Corp
- **Email:** alice@acme.com
- **Tags:** engineering, backend

**Content:**
> Alice is a senior backend engineer who joined Acme in 2025. She leads the Platform v2 migration effort.

**Connected to:**
- Acme Corp (org)
- Platform v2 (project)
- Deploy staging environment (task)
- Architecture Review (conversation)
</Accordion>

## How connections work

When Alfred processes a meeting note that mentions "Alice from Acme discussed the Q1 launch," it creates or updates multiple records and links them together:

- The **conversation** record links to Alice (person) and Q1 Launch (project)
- Alice's **person** record links back to the conversation and to Acme Corp (org)
- The **project** record links to Alice and the conversation

These bidirectional connections mean you can start from any record and navigate to everything related.

## The three layers

Your vault organizes knowledge into three layers:

### Who and what — Standing entities

Records for the stable elements of your world: people, organizations, projects, locations, accounts, assets, and processes. These persist and accumulate connections over time.

### What happened — Activity records

Records for events, conversations, tasks, notes, and sessions. These capture what occurred and link to the entities involved.

### What we know — Learning records

Records created by the Distiller that surface hidden knowledge: assumptions being made, decisions taken, constraints in play, contradictions between sources, and synthesized insights.

## How your vault grows

Every time you feed content to Alfred:

1. The Curator identifies entities and creates or updates records
2. New records are linked to existing ones where relevant
3. The Janitor ensures all connections are valid
4. The Distiller periodically extracts higher-order knowledge
5. The Surveyor clusters records semantically and discovers cross-record relationships

A single meeting note might create 5-10 new records and dozens of connections. Over weeks and months, your vault becomes an increasingly valuable knowledge base.

## Accessing your vault

You can browse and search your vault in two ways:

- **Dashboard** — Use the vault browser to filter by type, search by keyword, and click through connections
- **API** — Use endpoints like `GET /api/v1/vault/context`, `GET /api/v1/vault/search`, and `GET /api/v1/vault/list/{type}` for programmatic access

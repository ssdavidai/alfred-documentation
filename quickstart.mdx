## You don't need to do anything.

No API keys. No terminal commands. No configuration files. You sign up, and Alfred handles the rest — from provisioning your dedicated server to reading your first hundred days of email to writing your welcome briefing.

The entire onboarding takes about ten minutes of your time. Alfred works for hours behind the scenes.

<Note>
Alfred Black is a zero-config platform. Every new member gets a dedicated VPS (Hetzner cx53, 32GB RAM) provisioned automatically. You never touch infrastructure.
</Note>

---

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up with Google">
    Go to [alfred.black](https://alfred.black) and click **Sign up with Google**. Alfred uses Google OAuth to authenticate you and request access to your Gmail. The consent screen will ask for email read permissions — this is how Alfred begins learning about your world.

    No invite codes. No waitlists. One click.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Alfred provisions your dedicated instance">
    The moment you sign up, Alfred begins spinning up a dedicated VPS just for you. The dashboard shows a "Preparing your Alfred" animation while this happens — typically five to ten minutes.

    <Tip>
    You can watch the provisioning progress in real time. Every Alfred instance is isolated — your data never shares a server with anyone else's.
    </Tip>

    Behind the scenes: a Hetzner cx53 with 32GB of RAM is being configured, your vault database is being created, and your specialist workers are being initialised.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your apps">
    Before Alfred starts reading your world, you choose what to connect. The onboarding wizard shows three slots:

    - **Gmail** (required) — Alfred needs email access to build your initial profile. Click Connect, authorize in the popup, done.
    - **Google Calendar** (recommended) — calendar events give Alfred schedule awareness. One more popup click.
    - **One more app** (optional) — pick from 1,000+ apps: Notion, GitHub, Slack, Linear, Stripe, and more. Click "Add another app," search, connect.

    All connections use Composio's OAuth — credentials are stored server-side and never expire. You authorise once and Alfred maintains access permanently.

    <Tip>
    You can always add more apps later from the **Apps** page in the dashboard. But connecting during onboarding means Alfred has richer data to work with from day one.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="The 100-day Gmail backfill begins">
    Once your apps are connected and you click "Generate Your First Brief," Alfred starts reading your Gmail — going back 100 days. This is not a shallow scan. Alfred fetches full email content and extracts facts from every message, day by day.

    The dashboard shows an animated orb while Alfred works:

    > *"Alfred is coming to life. I'm reading your world now."*

    People, organisations, projects, decisions, commitments, deadlines — Alfred is building a structured understanding of your life from the raw material of your inbox.

    <Note>
    The backfill runs in the background. You do not need to keep the dashboard open. Alfred will keep working whether you watch or not.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pattern analysis and personalisation">
    As the backfill completes, Alfred's specialists begin deeper work:

    - **Pattern analysis** — identifying recurring themes, relationships, and rhythms in your communication
    - **Personalisation** — generating your `USER.md` (who you are, what you care about) and `SOUL.md` (how Alfred should speak to you, what tone fits)
    - **Automation suggestions** — spotting opportunities where Alfred can handle things without being asked
    - **First Brief composition** — drafting your butler-quality welcome letter
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fact verification">
    After patterns and personalisation complete, Alfred pauses before sharing its first observations. A **fact verification card** appears on screen:

    > *"Sir, before I share my first observations, I want to ensure I have the basics right..."*

    Alfred displays 8–12 key identity facts it extracted from your email — your name, age, location, partner, children, company, job title, and other core details. Each fact is **editable inline** — click any item to correct it.

    <Tip>
    Take a moment to review each fact. Corrections are fed directly into brief generation, ensuring Alfred never gets your identity basics wrong. This is the last step before your First Brief — it's worth getting right.
    </Tip>

    Once you confirm, Alfred incorporates your corrections and begins composing your personalised welcome briefing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Your First Brief appears">
    When Alfred is ready, your First Brief appears on screen. This is not a system notification or a generic welcome message. It is a personal letter — written by Alfred, about you, based on everything just learned from your email.

    It will mention people by name. It will reference real projects. It will surface something you forgot about. This is Alfred introducing itself by demonstrating that it already knows you.

    <Tip>
    The First Brief is the moment most members realise this is different from anything they have used before. Take a moment to read it properly.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Continue to your Dashboard">
    Click **Continue to Dashboard** to enter your Alfred. The first thing you see is the **Vault Nebula** — a real-time visualisation of your knowledge graph. People, organisations, projects, and facts, all connected and floating in space.

    Your Gmail stream is now active, pulling new email every five minutes. Your vault will grow continuously from here.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add more integrations">
    From the **Apps** page (puzzle icon in the sidebar), you can connect additional services from a catalog of over 1,000 apps:

    - **Notion** — sync your pages and databases automatically
    - **GitHub** — track notifications, PRs, and issues
    - **Slack** — post messages and interact with channels
    - **Linear, Stripe, Todoist, Asana** — and hundreds more

    Every new connection gives Alfred both **data** (via automatic streams) and **actions** (via the `composio_execute` tool). Connect Notion, and Alfred can read your pages. Connect Slack, and Alfred can post messages on your behalf.

    <Tip>
    Connected apps use Composio for persistent OAuth. Unlike the legacy integration flow, these tokens never expire — Alfred maintains access indefinitely without re-authentication.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## What just happened?

In roughly ten minutes of your time, Alfred:

1. Provisioned a dedicated server for you
2. Connected your apps via Composio (Gmail, Calendar, and more)
3. Read 100 days of your email
4. Extracted thousands of structured facts
5. Built a knowledge graph of your world
6. Analysed your patterns and relationships
7. Wrote you a personalised welcome briefing
8. Activated real-time streams for every connected app
9. Generated skill files so Alfred can take actions on your behalf
10. Started working — permanently

You didn't configure anything. You didn't write a single API call. You signed up, and Alfred did the rest.

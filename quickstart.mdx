## Five minutes from you. Three hours from Alfred.

No API keys. No terminal commands. No configuration files. You sign up, connect a few apps, and walk away. Alfred spends the next three hours reading your world, analysing your patterns, generating personalised automations, and writing you a welcome briefing.

The five minutes are yours: sign up, connect apps, verify a few facts. The three hours are Alfred's — a nine-stage intelligence pipeline that builds a complete model of your life before you touch the dashboard.

<Note>
Alfred Black is a zero-config platform. Every new member gets a dedicated VPS provisioned automatically on Hetzner Cloud. You never touch infrastructure. Your data never shares a server with anyone else's.
</Note>

---

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up with Google">
    Go to [alfred.black](https://alfred.black) and click **Sign up with Google**. Alfred uses Google OAuth to authenticate you and request access to your Gmail. The consent screen will ask for email read permissions — this is how Alfred begins learning about your world.

    No invite codes. No waitlists. One click.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Alfred provisions your dedicated instance">
    The moment you sign up, Alfred begins spinning up a dedicated VPS just for you. The dashboard shows a progress animation — typically five to ten minutes.

    <Tip>
    You can watch the provisioning progress in real time. Every Alfred instance is isolated — encrypted at rest, no shared infrastructure.
    </Tip>

    Behind the scenes: a dedicated Hetzner Cloud server is being configured, your vault is being created, and your specialist workers are being initialised.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your apps">
    Before Alfred starts reading your world, you choose what to connect. The onboarding wizard shows three slots:

    - **Gmail** (required) — Alfred needs email access to build your initial profile. Click Connect, authorize in the popup, done.
    - **Google Calendar** (recommended) — calendar events give Alfred schedule awareness. One more popup click.
    - **One more app** (optional) — pick from 1,000+ apps: Notion, GitHub, Slack, Linear, Stripe, and more. Click "Add another app," search, connect.

    All connections use Composio's OAuth — credentials are stored server-side and never expire. You authorise once and Alfred maintains access permanently.

    <Tip>
    You can always add more apps later from the **Apps** page in the dashboard. But connecting during onboarding means Alfred has richer data to work with from day one.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Alfred reads your world">
    Click **"Generate Your First Brief"** and the animated orb appears. This is where Alfred's work begins — a nine-stage pipeline that runs for roughly three hours:

    **Stage 1 — Email fetch** (1–2 min): Alfred reads the last 100 days of your Gmail. Not just subject lines — full emails, every thread, every sender.

    **Stage 2 — Behavioral profiler** (5–10 min): A pure machine-learning pipeline analyses your email patterns. No AI language model — just maths. It detects:
    - Your **daily rhythm** — when you start work, when you stop, how regular you are
    - Your **inner circle** — the people you communicate with most, ranked by frequency and reciprocity
    - Your **financial footprint** — subscription services, payment patterns, recurring charges
    - Your **communication style** — thread depth, response times, weekend activity
    - **Anomalies** — senders who disappeared, timing shifts, volume spikes

    This structured profile grounds everything that follows. When Alfred writes about you, it draws on real data — not guesses.

    **Stage 3 — Fact extraction** (1–2 min): A single Opus call extracts 200–400 structured facts from your emails, organised across ten categories: personal, home, health, professional, financial, social, routine, interests, travel, and services.

    **Stage 4 — Pattern discovery** (1–2 min): Another Opus call synthesises behavioral patterns from the facts and profiler data — work patterns, communication patterns, life patterns, priorities, and key relationships.

    **Stage 5 — Personalisation** (1–2 min): Opus writes your `USER.md` (who you are, what you care about) and `SOUL.md` (how Alfred should speak to you, what tone fits).

    <Note>
    The orb keeps animating while all of this runs. You don't need to keep the dashboard open — Alfred works whether you watch or not.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fact verification">
    After personalisation completes, Alfred pauses. A **fact verification card** appears on screen:

    > *"Sir, before I share my first observations, I want to ensure I have the basics right..."*

    Alfred displays 8–12 key identity facts — your name, age, location, partner, children, company, job title, and other core details extracted from your email. Each fact is **editable inline** — click any item to correct it.

    <Tip>
    Take a moment to review each fact. Corrections are fed directly into brief generation, ensuring Alfred never gets your identity basics wrong. This is the last step that requires your attention.
    </Tip>

    Once you confirm, the pipeline resumes automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Your First Brief and vault seeding">
    Three things happen after you confirm your facts:

    **The First Brief** — Opus composes a personal letter about you based on everything just learned. It mentions people by name, references real projects, and surfaces something you forgot about. This appears on screen as a butler-quality welcome letter.

    **Four packs** (runs in background, ~60 min) — While you read the brief, Alfred generates your initial vault population:
    - **Matter pack** — 8–12 ongoing concerns Alfred will track (career, family, health, finances, etc.)
    - **Instinct pack** — 8–12 learned routing rules based on your behavioral patterns
    - **Errand pack** — 8–12 one-time actionable items discovered from your emails
    - **Stream pack** — suggested app integrations based on detected service domains

    **Personalised chores** (runs in background, ~20 min) — Alfred generates 4–8 recurring automations tailored to your life. Each chore is a real Python workflow:
    - Written by Opus based on your profiler data and opportunities
    - Statically validated and smoke-tested in a sandbox
    - Deployed and scheduled immediately
    - First three runs are dry-run (quarantine) to catch errors before going live

    By the time you reach the dashboard, your vault has 40+ records, your chores are running, and your streams are polling.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Continue to your Dashboard">
    Click **Continue to Dashboard** to enter your Alfred. The first thing you see is the **Vault Nebula** — a real-time visualisation of your knowledge graph. People, organisations, projects, and facts, all connected and floating in space.

    Behind the scenes, Alfred also finalises your app connections — creating persistent Composio streams for every app you connected during onboarding, with incremental sync so only new data is fetched on each poll.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add more integrations">
    From the **Apps** page (puzzle icon in the sidebar), you can connect additional services from a catalog of over 1,000 apps:

    - **Notion** — sync your pages and databases automatically
    - **GitHub** — track notifications, PRs, and issues
    - **Slack** — post messages and interact with channels
    - **Linear, Stripe, Todoist, Asana** — and hundreds more

    Every new connection gives Alfred both **data** (via automatic streams) and **actions** (via the `composio_execute` tool). Connect Notion, and Alfred can read your pages. Connect Slack, and Alfred can post messages on your behalf.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## What just happened?

In five minutes of your time, you triggered a three-hour intelligence pipeline that:

1. Provisioned a dedicated encrypted server
2. Connected your apps via Composio (persistent OAuth, never expires)
3. Read 100 days of your email (up to 5,000 messages)
4. Ran a behavioral profiler — rhythm analysis, relationship mapping, financial detection, anomaly detection
5. Extracted 200–400 structured facts across ten life categories
6. Discovered behavioral patterns in your communication, work, and life
7. Wrote personalised configuration files (USER.md, SOUL.md)
8. Verified key facts with you and incorporated corrections
9. Composed a butler-quality welcome briefing
10. Generated 40+ vault records (matters, instincts, errands)
11. Created 4–8 personalised recurring automations (chores)
12. Activated incremental streams for every connected app
13. Started working — permanently

You didn't configure anything. You didn't write a single API call. You signed up, connected your apps, confirmed a few facts, and Alfred built a complete model of your world.

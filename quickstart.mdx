## What Sir does. What Alfred does.

Sir's role on day one: sign up, connect Gmail (and ideally Calendar plus one more app), confirm a short list of facts about Sir, then close the laptop. Alfred's role: read 100 days of email, build a behavioural profile, generate a personal welcome brief, populate the vault, write Sir bespoke recurring chores, and start working.

The whole pipeline runs unattended. Sir does not need to keep the dashboard open.

<Note>
Every new subscriber gets a dedicated VPS provisioned automatically on Hetzner Cloud. Sir's data never shares a machine with anyone else's. Sir never touches infrastructure, configuration files, or terminal commands.
</Note>

---

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up with Google">
    Go to [alfred.black](https://alfred.black) and click **Sign up with Google**. Authentication is OAuth; the consent screen asks for Gmail read access — that is how Alfred begins learning Sir's world.

    No invite codes. No waitlist.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Alfred provisions Sir's instance">
    The moment Sir signs up, a dedicated Hetzner Cloud server is requested in Sir's name. Disk encryption is enabled at boot. The dashboard shows a progress animation — typically five to ten minutes from click to ready.

    During this window the vault is initialised, the Tailscale mesh joins, the OpenClaw gateway boots, and the Temporal workflow engine starts. When the dashboard advances, Alfred is alive.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect Sir's apps">
    Before Alfred reads anything, Sir chooses what to connect. The wizard presents three slots:

    - **Gmail** (required) — Alfred needs email access to build the initial profile
    - **Google Calendar** (recommended) — adds schedule awareness from day one
    - **One more app** (optional) — Notion, GitHub, Slack, Linear, Stripe, and 1,000+ others via the catalog

    All connections route through Composio's managed OAuth. Credentials live in Composio's vault, not on Sir's tenant. Sir authorises once and the connection persists.

    <Tip>
    Sir can add or remove integrations at any time from the **Apps** page. Connecting more during onboarding gives Alfred richer ground for the first brief.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Alfred reads Sir's world">
    Sir clicks **"Generate Your First Brief"** and the orb appears. The pipeline runs through nine stages — Sir does not need to watch.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="1. Email metadata" icon="envelope">
        Headers and snippets from the last 100 days of Gmail are pulled into onboarding state. ~1–2 min.
      </Card>
      <Card title="2. Behavioural profiler" icon="chart-line">
        Pure Python — no LLM. Detects Sir's daily rhythm, inner circle, financial footprint, communication style, and anomalies. ~5–10 min.
      </Card>
      <Card title="3. Fact extraction" icon="list-check">
        One Opus call extracts 200–400 structured facts about Sir across ten life categories. ~1–2 min.
      </Card>
      <Card title="4. Pattern discovery" icon="diagram-project">
        Another Opus call synthesises behavioural patterns from the facts and the profile. ~1–2 min.
      </Card>
      <Card title="5. Personalisation" icon="user-pen">
        Opus writes Sir's `USER.md` (who Sir is, what matters) and `SOUL.md` (how Alfred should speak to Sir). ~1–2 min.
      </Card>
      <Card title="6. Verification pause" icon="hand">
        Workflow returns. Dashboard shows the fact verification card. Sir's turn.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

    <Note>
    The orb keeps animating throughout. Sir can close the tab — Alfred works whether or not Sir watches.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify the facts">
    Alfred pauses and presents 8–12 key identity facts — name, location, profession, household, partner, key projects, and so on, all extracted from email. Each is editable inline. Sir can correct anything Alfred misread.

    > *"Sir, before I share my first observations, I'd like to confirm I have the basics right..."*

    Once Sir confirms, the pipeline resumes. Corrections feed directly into brief generation so Alfred never gets the basics wrong from here on.
  </Step>

  <Step title="The First Brief and the four packs">
    Three things happen after Sir confirms.

    **The First Brief** — Opus composes a personal welcome letter referencing Sir by name, mentioning real people from Sir's correspondence, and surfacing something Sir likely forgot. It arrives both on screen and as an email to Sir's Google address. Sir's reply to that email establishes the `mail.alfred.black` channel for ongoing correspondence.

    **The four packs** (~10–15 min, in the background, while Sir reads the brief):
    - **Matter pack** — 8–12 ongoing concerns Alfred will track (career, family, health, finances)
    - **Instinct pack** — 8–12 learned routing patterns based on Sir's behaviour
    - **Errand pack** — 8–12 actionable items already discoverable in Sir's emails
    - **Stream pack** — suggested integrations based on services detected in Sir's mail

    **Personalised chores** (~15–20 min, also in the background) — Alfred generates 4–8 recurring workflows tailored to Sir. Each chore is a real Python workflow:
    - Authored by Opus from the profile and detected opportunities
    - Statically validated and smoke-tested in a sandbox
    - Deployed and scheduled automatically
    - First three runs are dry-run (quarantine) — no notifications, no vault writes — to catch errors before going live

    By the time Sir reaches the dashboard, the vault has 40+ records, chores are scheduled, and streams are polling.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Continue to the Dashboard">
    Click **Continue to Dashboard** and Sir lands on the **Vault Nebula** — the living visualisation of Sir's knowledge graph. People, projects, decisions, conversations, all clustered and floating.

    From here, Sir can:
    - Open the **Apps** page to connect more integrations
    - Open **Intelligence** to review Matters, Errands, Chores, and the Inbox
    - Open **Settings → Phone** to claim a dedicated phone number for SMS and voice
    - Hand Alfred a meeting note via the inbox and watch the Curator file it
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What just happened

In Sir's five minutes:

1. A dedicated, encrypted server was provisioned in Sir's name
2. Apps connected via Composio with persistent, never-expiring authorisation
3. 100 days of Sir's email read and analysed
4. A behavioural profile assembled — rhythm, relationships, finances, anomalies
5. 200–400 facts extracted across ten life categories
6. Behavioural patterns synthesised
7. `USER.md` and `SOUL.md` personalised
8. Fact verification offered to Sir
9. A personal welcome brief composed and emailed
10. 40+ vault records seeded across matters, instincts, errands
11. 4–8 bespoke recurring chores generated, validated, and scheduled
12. Incremental streams activated for every connected app

Sir wrote no code, configured nothing. Sir signed up, connected apps, confirmed facts, and closed the laptop.

## The first 24 hours

| When | What's happening |
|------|------------------|
| **Minute 0–5** | Server provisioning, Tailscale, vault scaffold |
| **Minute 5–15** | Email pull, behavioural profile, fact verification offered |
| **Minute 15–60** | First Brief delivered, packs and chores generated in the background |
| **Hour 1+** | Streams polling on schedule (Gmail every few minutes, Calendar via delta sync) |
| **Hour 1+** | Hourly enrichment batches recent records — entities, tags, context — in a single LLM call |
| **Hour 6** | Daily digest workflow scheduled for 6pm local |
| **Hour 24** | Nightly reflection at 2am refines instincts from the day's observations |
